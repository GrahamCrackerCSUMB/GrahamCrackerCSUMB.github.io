Event Description
Sprint planning A meeting where the team figures out what they want to do in the next Sprint. The product owner discusses what the goal of the sprint is, and then developers select items for the backlog to accomplish this sprint. The team then make a plan for what they want to accomplish. This can often be done by breaking down larger project or goals into smaller projects.
Sprint A sprint is a fixed amount of time, for instance, two weeks or a month. Sprints occur repetitively, one right after another. You can consider each Sprint a short project.
Daily Scrum A daily meeting of around 15 minutes. It focuses on progress towards whatever the goal is for the sprint and create an actionable plan for the next day of work.
Sprint Review A meeting where you inspect the outcome of the Sprint. Developers demonstrate their work and evaluate progress towards the ultimate goal. This is a working session and everyone collaborates on what to do next.
Sprint Retrospective The team examines how the last sprint went and discusses what went well, what went wrong, and any problem that arose. This is a chance to think about helpful changes to be more effective in future sprints.
